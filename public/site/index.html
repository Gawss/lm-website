Augmented Reality through a

Heritage Experience

  • Organization : Full-Body Interaction Lab
  • Professor : Narcís Parés
  • Duration : 1 year
  • Technologies : ARkit, Unity, Python, Blender
  • Overview : Our project proposed the integration of the "Magic Lantern" as a World-as-Support (WaS) tool to improve the group and explorative experience within the Museu d’Història de Barcelona (MUHBA).
  • Description : The WaS paradigm has been introduced in previous researchs based on projective AR and embodied interaction within collaborative and educational environments, therefore, the proposal was presented as a continuation of the novel paradigm definition made by Dr. Marie-Monique SchaperI et al. In the first phase, we devised the state of the art related to traditional AR interaction paradigms (smartphone-based, Window-on-the-World - WoW) to be compared against WaS. Next, brainstorming and design workshops were executed in the Lab to define the core interactions. Then, we scanned the museum, turned it into a 3D model, and made a simulation of the journey; including in-game data recollection plus Likert scale questions and multimodal surveys. In the second phase, I analyzed the behavioral and declarative data to expose the research findings.
  • Application : The comparison of emerging AR interaction paradigms aims to understand and optimze the ways of manipulating digital elements within collaborative experiences.

Samsong

  • Position : Co-Founder, Developer
  • Duration : 5 Months
  • Technologies : Unity, Arduino
  • Overview : Samsong is a tower defense game borned in the Global Game Jam 2017.
